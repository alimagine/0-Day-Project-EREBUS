About the Project

Declassified excerpt — Project EREBUS is a one-time interactive terminal experience. You are the operator: a system technician assigned to a failing launch facility. The system is old, the logs are corrupt, and the orders are absolute. Operate the terminal. Configure weapons. Decide who lives. There are no resets.

Platform: Windows, Linux (native builds)
Engine: Godot 4.x (prototyping)
Session: Single-run (30–90 minutes)
Endings: 3 — choices are permanent

Key Features

  • Realistic Terminal Ops — Interact with an authentic-feeling control terminal; sequence components, match serials, and troubleshoot live systems.
  • Audio-as-Data — Ambient mechanical cues, log transmissions, and layered sound serve as gameplay clues.
  • No Reset Philosophy — Your choices alter the permanent state. The game treats every playthrough as consequential.
  • Three Distinct Endings — Each ending reflects your operational ethics.
  • Short, Dense Sessions — Designed to deliver high emotional impact in limited time.

Story Premise

Year: 2134. Deep beneath the frozen northern plate, a command facility controls converging weapon systems designed for absolute deterrence. The facility’s systems are failing. Corruption in logs begins to read like memory. You have one account, one session, one set of keys. Orders arrive. Some are obvious. Others are not. The machine will ask you to decide who the target is — you will not always be told what you hit.

Gameplay

Inspect files, configure weapon components, run diagnostics, and choose confirmations under pressure. The core loop: Inspect → Configure → Confirm → Observe. Some puzzle solves require cross-referencing logs with audio cues; others force you to choose under moral stress. There's no save/reload mechanic — every choice persists.

Puzzle types: sequence matching, timing windows, logic diagnostics.
Interface: terminal commands, point-&-click panels, quick troubleshooting minigames.
Audio: environmental hums, distant impact reports, encrypted voice logs.
